Cooking

Living in my own apartment off campus has forced me to become better at cooking for myself consistently. Especially now that all my classes are online, I don't go to campus nearly as much as I did previously. Thus I eat pretty much all my meals at home.

I've been a vegetarian my whole life so I don't cook with meat. I eat a lot of stir fry and pasta dishes but always try to pick different recipes to try out. Some of my recent favorite recipes have been a Sundried Tomato and Gnocchi dish, Spicy Kale Potato Bowls, and Thai Green Curry. I don't really have a favorite type of cuisine. Pretty much just make whatever I have the ingredients for! If you have any recipes you've made recently you feel so inclined to share I would really appreciate it. I'm always looking for new thing!

Houseplants

I've worked at a garden center for the past three summers and to say that it has fueled my obsession with houseplants would be an understatement. I'm pretty sure it started it. If I had to estimate I would say I have over 30 plants (not all in my apartment though).

I like watching them grow and searching for new ones that I don't have yet. When I first started buying plants I didn't know a whole lot, just bought ones that I thought were pretty. Once a few died on me I started doing more research and now I have more knowledge about houseplants than I ever thought I would. There's always more to learn and I love finding new plants that I didn't know existed. I had no idea how expensive some rare houseplants can get (upwards of $300 for just a cutting). I'm not sure if I trust my plant parenting skills yet to buy one of those.

Books

I started reading again for enjoyment for the first time since grade school two summers ago. I was taking a longer plane ride and wanted something to do that wasn't staring at my phone. I picked up The Outsider by Stephen King and barely put it down until I finished it. Since then I've read over 30 books, squeezing as many as I can in each break.

My two most recent finishes are The Guest List by Lucy Foley and A Thousand Splendid Suns by Khaled Hosseini. Both 5 stars in my mind. I already have a list waiting for me for this summer but am always looking for new things to add to it (please give me recommendations). I have a Kindle so I usually read books I've borrowed from the library virtually but I also have begun to visit used book stores if I just want to browse. Sometimes you just can't beat reading a paperback or hardcover.
